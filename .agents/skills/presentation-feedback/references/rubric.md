# Presentation Feedback Rubric

## Pacing
| Score | Words per minute | Guidance |
|-------|-----------------|----------|
| Excellent | 120–160 wpm | Comfortable for audiences |
| Good | 160–180 wpm | Slight slow-down for key points recommended |
| Too fast | > 180 wpm | Audience loses comprehension |
| Too slow | < 100 wpm | Risks losing engagement |

Acceptable exceptions: intentional slow-down for emphasis, pauses after transitions.

## Filler Words
Count: *um, uh, like, you know, so, actually, basically, literally, right, I mean*

| Score | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|
| Excellent | 0–1 per minute |
| Good | 2–3 per minute |
| Needs work | 4–5 per minute |
| Poor | > 5 per minute |

Tip: a 1–2 second silent pause is always preferable to a filler word.

## Tone
- **Strong**: varied pitch, direct statements, confident word choice, no hedging
- **Natural**: conversational but clear; slight informality is fine
- **Hesitant**: rising intonation at sentence ends, hedging language ("maybe", "kind of", "I think")
- **Monotone**: no pitch variation; high disengagement risk after 60 seconds

## Relevance to Slide
- **On-topic**: ≥ 80% of content directly addresses the current slide
- **Partial**: 50–80% on-topic; minor tangents
- **Off-topic**: < 50% on-topic; needs restructuring

Penalize heavily for: reading slide text verbatim (adds no value), covering next-slide content prematurely.
